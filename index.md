## 🎯 About This Application

MoodPlaylist creates music mixes based on your current feelings. You select your mood, and the software builds a playlist for you. It scans your local music library or connects to your streaming accounts to find tracks that match your state of mind. Use this tool to save time when you curate music for work, rest, or exercise.

## ⚙️ System Requirements

This application runs on Windows 10 and Windows 11. Your computer needs at least 4GB of RAM to run the music scanning process. Ensure you have 200MB of free disk space before you start the installation. You also need an active internet connection to sync your music accounts.

## 🎧 Picking Your First Mood

When the application opens, it displays a dashboard with different moods. Click on a mood category to begin. The software analyzes your library and builds a preview of the playlist. You can add or remove songs from this list before you save it to your music player. 

You can categorize moods as:

* Focused: Instrumental tracks for deep work.
* Energetic: Upbeat songs for sports or activity.
* Calm: Slow melodies for rest.
* Happy: High-tempo songs for a social setting.

Ensure your firewall settings permit the application to access the internet. If you cannot sync your music, check your sign-in details in the Settings menu. ## 📝 Frequently Asked Questions

Does this tool store my password?
No. The application uses a secure token to communicate with your music service. It does not see or save your login details.

Can I move the playlist to my phone?
Yes. Once the playlist saves, you can sync it with your mobile device through your music service app.

How often does the app update?
The app checks for updates automatically whenever you launch it. If a new version exists, a window appears on your screen with instructions to install it. 

Where does the app get the mood data?
The software uses a database of acoustic metadata to identify the tempo, key, and style of your songs. It labels each track based on these traits so you can group them by feeling.

Does this work without an internet connection?
The app needs an internet connection to pull album art and sync your cloud music library. You can listen to your saved playlists offline if you download the music files to your computer first. 

Can I change the mood labels?
The settings menu allows you to rename moods or create new tags for different emotions. You can map your own tracks to these custom tags through the library tab. 

Does this affect my original music files?
No. The software only reads your music files to create playlists. It does not modify, move, or delete any of your original files on your hard drive. 

What if the app does not find my music?
Go to Settings, then click Library Locations. Add the folders where you save your music. The application will scan those folders and include the songs in your search results. 

Is this software free to use?
Yes. This application is free for all users. You can download, install, and share it without restrictions. 

How do I remove the app?
Open your Windows Control Panel. Select Programs and Features. Find MoodPlaylist in the list, right-click it, and select Uninstall. This removes all program files from your system.
